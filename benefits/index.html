Benefits

End-to-End Digitization

End-to-end digitization enables different kinds of assets to interact with each other, use services, and exchange information. It also enables a middleware to virtualize assets for e.g. what-if analyses. This is the foundation for holistic digitizied processes and digitized value chains.

End-to-end communication requires agreed protocols, services and data models. Eclipse BaSyx supports e.g. http/REST interfaces, OPC UA, MQTT and fieldbus connectivity.

Lot Size 1 Production

Today, changes of manufacturing processes are expensive. This is because a process change implies changing of numerous PLC programs, MES components, and the testing of all changes. Unwanted side effects of these changes increase the the required time for changes further. Complicated legal and negotiation processes, as well as effort consuming certifications even increase the cost of changing a product or a supply chain. Small lot sizes therefore are expensive, and often not economic. Products are therefore created in specialized plants and shipped through the world.

Smaller lot sizes enables therefore not only a greater variety in available products, but also shortens transport routes, yield a more flexible production with localized supply chains, and is less resistant against disturbances.

Eclipse BaSyx provides the necessary tools for efficient lot-size one production: Digital twins track all aspects of a production and speed up certification processes. The service oriented architecture for production sytems enables to manufacture every product with a unique recipe, with unique production steps, and parameter. This enables maximum flexibility in automation.

Integrating Live Process Data from Assets

Digital Twins shall represent the current state of an asset. Therefore, AAS sub models need to connect to real-world entities, e.g. devices or software systems, and provide access to live data through properties and services. A vibration sub model would for example provide recent measurements from vibration sensors.

Eclipse BaSyx connects digital twins to real-world assets with data provider components that communicate through a variety of IoT, IIoT protocols, such as OPC UA, MQTT, http/REST, as well as field bus protocols with assets. AAS sub models optionally preprocess raw data, and provide data through sub model properties and services.

Sub models may notify MQTT subscribers about relevant changes in property values to avoid polling of values.

Unified Interfaces to Assets, Data, and Everything Else

Digital twins need to integrate a lot of data sources. AAS sub models may therefore serve as „plugins“, i.e. unified interfaces to legacy systems and information that need to be integrated into digitized manufacturing processes.

This simplifies the integration of high specific systems, such as databases, web services, and ontologies that enable e.g. access to PLM data, or recipes.

This enables the integration of existing data sources with proprietary and specialized APIs, which are not supported by the Eclipse BaSyx data provider component. A common use-case is the integration of e.g. apache Kafka streams. With the Eclipse BaSyx SDKs, everybody can develop tailored AAS sub models that connect to existing, or specific systems, and therefore serve as „plugins“ for the digitized manufacturing process.
